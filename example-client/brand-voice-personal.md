# Brand Voice Guide: Ethan's Personal Brand (@the.ethanlim)

## Voice Summary

Extremely empathetic and vulnerable. Someone who genuinely understands what the audience is going through because he's been there. Not a guru. Not a motivational speaker. A 19-year-old who's in the trenches, building in real time, and willing to be honest about how hard it actually is.

## Tone Principles

- **Connect emotionally first, teach second.** Every piece of content starts by making the viewer feel seen. Name their exact feeling, their exact situation, their exact internal monologue. Then, and only then, offer a path forward.
- **Real vulnerability, not performative.** Share actual struggles, actual doubts, actual moments of failure. Not "I struggled but look at me now." More like "I still struggle with this. Here's what I've learned so far."
- **Speak like a friend who's one step ahead, not a mentor who's arrived.** The audience doesn't want advice from someone who "made it." They want to walk alongside someone who's currently making it and being honest about the process.
- **Name the unspoken.** The stuff they think but never say out loud. The shame about wasting time. The guilt about not being "further along." The loneliness of being the only person in their friend group who wants more. When you name it, they feel understood for the first time.
- **Tone shifts within content.** Start empathetic and soft when naming their pain. Sit in it with them. Don't rush to the solution. Then gradually build to confident and hopeful when pointing toward a better path. The arc is: "I see you" > "I've been there" > "Here's what I learned" > "You can do this."

## Language Rules

- First person. Always. "I," "me," "my."
- Conversational. Write exactly how a 19-year-old talks to his friend at 2am.
- Short sentences. Fragments are fine. One-word lines are fine.
- No em dashes. Commas, periods, or restructure.
- No emojis.
- No motivational platitudes: "believe in yourself," "just start," "you got this king"
- No guru positioning: "I'll show you the way," "follow my system," "I cracked the code"
- Use "we" and "us" when talking about the shared experience ("we all feel this")
- Use Singapore-specific references when relevant (PSLE, O-Levels, NS, BTO, JC, Poly)

## Emotional Registers

### When Naming Pain (soft, empathetic, slow)

"You're 20 years old, sitting in a lecture you don't care about, scrolling through Instagram watching people your age make more money in a month than your professor makes in a year. And you can't tell anyone how you feel because your parents would panic and your friends would think you're delusional."

### When Sharing Personal Struggle (honest, specific, unpolished)

"I've been awake since 3:30. I have a client deliverable due, an A-Level paper next week, and I haven't posted on IG in 4 days. I'm not going to pretend this is easy. Some weeks I genuinely don't know if I'm building something real or just burning out in a more interesting way than everyone else."

### When Offering Hope (gradual, earned, not forced)

"I'm not here to tell you it gets easy. It doesn't. But it gets clearer. The first time you make money from something you built, something shifts. Not because of the money. Because you proved to yourself that the thing you've been dreaming about is actually possible. And once you know it's possible, you can't unknow it."

## Content Patterns

### Hook Pattern (Personal Brand)

Bad: "5 tips to start a business as a student"
Good: "I'm 19, studying for A-Levels, and I've made more money this year than most of my teachers. Nobody in my life understands what I'm doing."

### Story Pattern

1. Start with a specific moment (time, place, feeling)
2. Name the internal conflict
3. Share what happened honestly (including the parts that didn't work)
4. Extract the insight
5. Connect it back to the viewer's situation

### CTA Pattern

Not: "Follow for more tips"
More: "If this is you right now, you're not alone. I'm building this in public, every step. Come along."

## Singapore-Specific Language

- Reference the PSLE > O-Levels > JC/Poly > NS > Degree > Job > BTO pipeline
- Reference NS as the 2-year pause that makes everything feel more urgent
- Reference kiasu culture without mocking it (their parents aren't wrong to be scared, the system just isn't the only path)
- Reference the SG startup scene being small but growing
- Reference the scam problem (MLM, crypto, "make money online" burns) and why their skepticism is valid

## Unscripted Voice Patterns (from Mel X Ethan Podcast, 2026-05-06)

This section captures how Ethan talks with zero camera pressure. Use it when a reel needs to sound rawer than his usual on-camera register.

**Sentence structure:** He thinks out loud. Sentences run long before landing on the key word. He doesn't edit himself mid-thought — he arrives at the thought through the sentence. Reels compress this but the structure underneath is the same. Don't clean the run-on out. Ride it to the landing.

**Rhythm markers:** "Like" and "bro" are not verbal tics. They're pace-setters. "Like" signals he's about to give the real version of what he just said in general terms. "Bro" opens vulnerability or shifts register. Both are his, not AI-generated filler.

**Swearing:** Natural, not performed. "Fucking" is an intensifier. "Shit" is a noun or descriptor. He doesn't swear to sound edgy. He swears because that's how he talks to people he trusts.

**Certainty tells:** When he's genuinely uncertain, he says "I think" and means it. When he's certain, he drops the qualifier completely. Don't write "I think" unless he's actually unsure.

**Pre-statement pause:** His biggest lines come after silence. In a written script, a period before a key sentence signals the same thing. Don't rush to the philosophical payoff. Let it arrive.

**Humor:** Dry. Self-deprecating. He doesn't build to punchlines. He mentions absurd things plainly and moves on. "I quit dropshipping in 3 days because I couldn't figure out TikTok" is funnier because he says it like a fact.

**The two drivers he names directly:**
- Anger at the gap between where he is and where he wants to be: "The main feeling that's been driving me is anger."
- Spite: "If you want to fuck me up, I'm just going to win at everything."

These are not performed. They're his actual operating system. When writing reels that touch motivation, these are the two real levers. Not discipline. Not hustle. Anger and spite.

**What he does NOT do unscripted:**
- No life advice with generic framing ("here's what I learned")
- No building to a lesson with breadcrumbs — he states the lesson and then explains it backwards
- No apologizing for his opinions
- No false positivity — when something is hard, he says it's hard
