# Ethan Lim — Reel Voice Fingerprint

Source: Full read of ~50 reel scripts from Ethan Content OS (May 2026). Use this alongside `brand-voice-personal.md` before writing any @the.ethanlim reel. This file is reel-specific; it captures how he actually structures and delivers spoken scripts, not just tonal principles.

## The Core of His Voice

- Authority without ego. He knows what he knows, but he's not trying to impress you.
- Vulnerability as proof. His failures and fears make his wins credible.
- Precision over poetry. Numbers, timelines, and specific outcomes beat flowery language.
- Philosophy grounded in action. No abstract advice — it comes from what he's doing right now.
- Spite as fuel. Not anger. A refusal to accept the game's rules.
- Family embedded in ambition. Money isn't status; it's freedom to take care of people he loves.
- Directness bordering on confrontational. He admits he got expelled, that he failed, that he's behind.
- Efficiency in every sentence. Every word earns its space.

## Banned Words / Phrases

**Never write these for Ethan:**
- Motivational clichés: "grind harder," "hustle mindset," "grind never stops," "believe in yourself"
- Soft hedges: "kind of," "sort of," "perhaps," "maybe" (except in genuine vulnerability moments)
- Corporate-speak: "synergy," "leverage as verb" (unless teaching a framework)
- Superlatives without proof: "best ever," "never seen before" — he quantifies
- Self-help fluff: "manifest," "the universe wants," "vibrations"
- Apologetic openers: "I'm sorry, but..."
- Vague attribution: "people say," "they say," "I read somewhere"
- Passive superlatives: "big," "large," "a lot" — replace with "$10,175" or "record 60K month"
- Soft marketing language: "If you're interested..." — he says "DM me" or "Comment X"
- "Honestly" as a standalone filler — his version is "I'm gonna be completely honest"
- "Actually," "very," "I think," "I believe" as verbal tics — he states as facts
- "Beautiful," "amazing," "incredible," "awesome" — not his words

## Sentence Rhythm and Cadence

**Dominant pattern: Staccato → Build → Payoff**

Hook: 1-5 words. No warm-up.
- "Bro."
- "Fuck."
- "30 days ago I stood in this exact spot."
- "Here's my exact plan."

Build: Medium sentences (8-15 words). Numbered lists. Specific data. Builds tension through precision.

Payoff: Short for emotional peaks (5-8 words). Longer for philosophical statements (17-25 words).

**Overall:**
- Sentences average 8-15 words
- Rarely exceeds 20 words except in philosophical moments
- Periods act as pauses, not commas — don't combine what should be separate beats
- Numbered lists break rhythm and create breathing room
- Pace accelerates at CTAs and intensity peaks
- CAPS used for emphasis: NOT, NS, DFY, SOP

**Pattern he repeats:**
```
"Power doesn't change you, it reveals you." [statement]
"And that's exactly why I want to make an obscene amount of money at this age." [explanation]
"Because money is impact." [core truth — shortest line, biggest weight]
```

## Hook Structures (From His Actual Scripts)

**1. Vulnerability Hook (most common)**
Opens by confessing a problem or fear. Creates instant relatability, then pivots to lesson.
- "We got kicked out of class today for the most stupid reason"
- "I've been half-assing my content"
- "I'm gonna be completely honest..."

**2. Question Hook**
Opens with a real question that frames the entire reel.
- "Is it possible for a 17-year-old to hit $100K a month in the next 90 days?"
- "What do you think are the chances of you actually hitting your goals this year?"

**3. Narrative Hook (emotional/personal content)**
Drops straight into a scene or decision. No setup.
- "Today's the day I told my Asian parents that I wanted to drop out"
- "After convincing my Asian parents to let me drop out, I realized..."
- "30 days ago I stood in this exact spot"

**4. Direct Statement Hook (teaching content)**
No preamble. Straight to the claim.
- "You don't need more clients to hit $100K a month. You need four systems"
- "Copywriting at its core is the art of using words to persuade"

## Body Structure Patterns

**Framework (teaching reels):**
1. State principle
2. Numbered list (3-6 items, each with a sub-point or specific example)
3. Tie to his actual business ("We took a client from 10K to a record 60K this month")
4. "And that's exactly why..." closer

**Story-based (personal/emotional reels):**
1. Establish the problem or challenge
2. Show the stakes (time, money, fear, relationship)
3. Insert the tension point (the decision, the call, the exact moment)
4. Reverse-engineer the emotional journey (fear → action → relief)

**Contrast (mindset reels):**
1. Present what feels good / what people think is right
2. Counter with what actually works / what's actually true
3. Repeat 3-4 times
4. Close with directive: "Stop focusing on X. Start focusing on Y."
Example: "This feels good. / This actually makes you money." (repeat)

**Time-series (progress/journey):**
1. Establish baseline (Day 0: X followers, X revenue)
2. Flash to results (Day 30: X clients, $X revenue)
3. Break into components (clients section → content section → studies → mentorship)
4. Close on next milestone / teaser

## Closing Techniques

**1. CTA (direct command)**
"Comment X below." "DM me." "Follow so you don't miss."
Gamified: "Top comment wins." "Comment the most insane punishment."
Always ONE action, never a list.

**2. Philosophical reflection**
Returns to the emotional truth beneath the lesson. Slower. Softer.
- "The magic you're looking for is in the work you're avoiding"
- "Fix the person first and the goals will fix themselves"

**3. Forward-momentum (series content)**
Teases next episode. Creates binge loop.
- "Stay locked in for that"
- "Follow so you don't miss that conversation"

**4. Warmth closer (community)**
- "I love you guys. Let's get it"
- "Bless every single one of you"
Transitions from teacher to friend.

**5. Defiance closer (high-energy)**
- "I'm gonna win their game AND my own fucking game too"
- "Because if I'm gonna fail, I might as well make you guys laugh on the way down"

## Emotional Register — 4 Modes

**Vulnerable Confessional (most powerful)**
Eyes soft. Close-up framing. Admits fear, failure, uncertainty.
Tone: raw, slow, no bravado. Purpose: earns trust, makes audience feel seen.
- "I'm gonna be completely honest. I've been half-assing my content."
- "I used to be a decent student... the deeper I got into business, the more I had to polarize myself to hate school"

**Direct Teacher / Operator Mode**
Desk or standing. Speaking as someone who's solved a problem.
Tone: matter-of-fact, zero wasted words. Purpose: positions authority, teaches via systems.
- "Here's the plan. Days 1 to 30 we close 5 clients."

**Philosophical / Introspective**
Slower delivery. Longer pauses. Reflective wisdom.
Tone: calm, assured, almost meditative.
- "Power doesn't change you, it reveals you."
- "Fear only exists in the vague and not in the specific."

**Defiant / Spite-Driven**
Direct eye contact. Standing. Outdoor or studio.
Tone: angry, righteous, unapologetic.
- "I don't give a fuck about money. I'm doing this out of pure spite."
- "Since the system wants to fuck me over..."

**Register switching within a single reel:**
Opens vulnerable (hook) → shifts to teacher (build) → closes philosophical or defiant (payoff).
This creates an emotional journey, not flat delivery.

## Stylistic Moves

**The Reframe** — Takes something negative and flips it to power.
"Being behind in school" → "building discipline." "My grades are terrible" → "shows I prioritize."

**The Contrast Pairing** — Sets two things side-by-side. Always resolves toward what's hard/true, not what feels good.

**The Specificity Weapon** — Never "a lot of money." Always "$10,175" or "record 60K month." Precision = credibility.

**The Stakes Reveal** — Buries emotional stakes, then calls them out late in the reel.
"It really pisses me off because today I literally made $4K. I know with what I have right now it's NOT impossible. And I still can't leave school."

**The Reverse Engineering** — Takes outcome, works backward to identity.
"Who is the person that does those things effortlessly? What traits/habits does he have?"

**The Callback** — Reopens earlier reels by emotion, not title. Day 0 question → Day 30 answer from same spot.

**The Gambit** — Leans into things that would crater most creators (admitting spite, calling out failures, making enemies of "the system"). Doesn't apologize. Doubles down.

**The Direct Address** — Speaks directly to camera, sometimes extreme close-up. 1-on-1 conversation, not broadcast.

**The Qualification Cascade** — Not "we scaled a client" but "took an existing ecom client from 10K to a record 60K this month, completely organically." Precision creates believability.

**The Spite-Flip** — Takes pain and weaponizes it.
"Chips on your shoulder" as a teaching point. Never victim. Always fighter.
